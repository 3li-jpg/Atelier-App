## 3. Notice-and-takedown process

If you believe that content accessible through Atelier infringes your copyright or other IP rights, you may submit a takedown notice. To be valid, a notice must include:

1. **Identification of the work** claimed to have been infringed (or, if multiple works, a representative list).
2. **Identification of the infringing material** — the specific session, repository, account, or URL where the material is located, with enough detail for us to locate it.
3. **Your contact information** — name, address, telephone number, and email.
4. **A good-faith statement** that you believe the use is not authorized by the rights holder, its agent, or the law.
5. **A statement of accuracy and authority** — that the information in the notice is accurate and, under penalty of perjury, that you are the owner or authorized to act on the owner's behalf.
6. **Your physical or electronic signature.**

Send notices to **ip@studioatelier.ca**. We aim to acknowledge receipt within a reasonable period and to act on valid notices as described below. `[LEGAL REVIEW: set and commit to specific acknowledgement and action timelines (e.g. acknowledge within X business days).]`

## 4. What we do on a valid notice

On receipt of a valid notice, we will review it and, where appropriate, disable access to the identified material, suspend the relevant session or account, and/or remove the content. We will notify the affected user where practicable. Reports may also be submitted through the in-product abuse-reporting flow, which routes to the same team.

## 5. Counter-notice and restoration

If you believe your content was removed or disabled by mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notice including:

1. Identification of the material that was removed and the location at which it appeared.
2. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you have a good-faith belief the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.
3. Your name, address, telephone number, and email.
4. A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the relevant court (see Section 8) and will accept service of process from the party who filed the original notice or that party's agent.
5. Your physical or electronic signature.
